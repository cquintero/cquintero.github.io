About LearningSabbatical.com

‍

Hey! 

‍

I'm Chris. I created this site while finishing a learning sabbatical in the Fall of 2020 as a way to ease some of the pain points felt during my own learning journey.

‍

I have a few goals for the site: 

‍

  1. Help normalize the idea of a learning sabbatical. Grad school is socially acceptable to parents, friends, and employers. Self-study less so. Because of this, many people go to grad school as a way to transition from job to job even though it's expensive and may not actually teach you what you want to learn. Maybe those planning a learning journey can send LearningSabbatical.com to friends/family to help them understand what it is they're about to embark on.
  2. Elevate meta-learning resources. Getting better at learning how to learn added huge leverage to my learning journey.
  3. Elevate good process. Learning Sabbaticals work best when they have clear goals, feedback loops, and accountability. Without these, it's easy to waste time or get trapped by confirmation bias.
  4. Create community. While learning content is now free/easy to access, mentors and peers are not. Peers help with accountability, motivation, and social isolation. Mentors suggest resources and critique deliverables.

‍

If you have suggestions about the site or want to chat about self-directed learning, I'd love to speak with you. Feel free to reach out to me via Chris (at) learningsabbatical.com or schedule a 30min call here.
