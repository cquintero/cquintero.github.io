Goals for LearningSabbatical.com

  1. Normalize the idea of a learning sabbatical. Grad school is socially acceptable to parents, friends, and employers. Self-study less so. Because of this, many people go to grad school as a way to transition from job to job even though it's expensive and may not actually teach them what they want to learn. Learningsabbatical.com should be something people can send to their friends and family to help them understand what it is they're doing.
  2. Elevate meta learning resources. Conventional learning environments don't take into account what we know about how the brain works. Lectures / books are not optimized for maximal learning. Getting better at learning how to learn will add leverage to whatever your spend your time on.
  3. Elevant good process. Sabbaticals work best when they have clear goals, feedback loops, and accountability. Behavioral hacks > willpower.
  4. Elevate learning resources. Although we're drowning in content, great resources can be hard to find. "Growth Hacking" has made it difficult to discern the quality of books on Amazon and courses on edtech platforms. Learners need help finding the best resources on a given subject.
  5. Create community. While content is free/easy to access, mentors and peers are not. Peers help with accountability, motivation, and social isolation. Mentors suggest resources and critique deliverables.
