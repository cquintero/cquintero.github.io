General Tips / Advice

  • Treat memory as a conscious act and use spaced repetition systems like Anki.  When reading books, take notes or use Kindle's highlight functionality to capture important passages. Highlights get automatically uploaded here.  Review the highlights when you're finished with the book → synthesize the important concepts in your own words → create flashcards and review them in Anki.
  • Learning styles (auditory, visual, tactile, etc) are a total myth. Read Stanislas Dehaene's How We Learn for a high-level overview of the cognitive science of learning. Learn how to Learn on Coursera is also OK.
  • When you begin to research a new subject, search for predicates (someone who has gone on a similar learning journey before you and written about it). Gather and compare curriculums. Search for roadmaps/guides. Then, build your own map as you go to help organize the concepts you encounter.
  • When evaluating books, only read 3-star reviews. They tend to have a much higher signal to  noise ratio.
  • If the book, course, or project you selected isn't engaging you, drop it and find a better one. I went through 5 different statistics courses before I found some I liked.
  • Find an accountability buddy and schedule a weekly call to 1) Review the prior week's tasks 2) Review your daily habits and 3) Set new goals for the coming week. Choose one task to wager $$ on if you fail to complete it. If you don't have an accountability buddy, try using Stickk.
  • Consider tracking your routines / habits. I built this spreadsheet for tracking mine. Please copy it. Atomic Habits by James Clear is a great resource for behavior change.
